Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man Of India

Timeline of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

  • 1931: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Graduated from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, with a degree in Physics.
  • 1955: Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist.
  • 1960s-1970s: Played a significant role in India's space program, particularly in the development of SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle) and the successful launch of Rohini-1, India's first satellite in 1980.
  • 1982-1999: Served as the Chief Executive of Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP), which led to the development of missiles like Prithvi, Agni, Akash, Trishul, and Nag.
  • 1992-1999: Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organization.
  • 2002-2007: Served as the 11th President of India, elected with overwhelming support from both the ruling party and the opposition.
  • 2006: Conferred with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.
  • 2015: Passed away on July 27, 2015, after suffering a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was not just a scientist or a president; he was a true embodiment of humility, wisdom, and inspiration. His life and work continue to inspire generations, reminding us of the power of dedication, innovation, and selflessness.
